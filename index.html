About

I started my academic journey by pursuing a Bachelor's in Industrial Engineering, at ETSEIB, UPC, Barcelona, graduating in 2018. At the end of the degree, I enrolled in an optative robotics subject that awoke my deep interest in robotics. This made me continue my studies at the same institution, obtaining a Master's in Automatic Control and Robotics in 2020. Right after this, I worked for a year as a research assistant in the Perception and Manipulation laboratory at the Institut de Robòtica i Informàtica Industrial, CSIC-UPC. After two years in the industry sector, where I applied artificial intelligence systems to the industry, I came back to the academy. In June 2023, I started my Ph.D. study in robotics at the Institut de Robòtica i Informàtica Industrial de Barcelona (IRI). My research focuses on the personalization and adaptation of robots in Human-Robot Interaction. The thesis is supervised by Dr. Antonio Andriella and Dr. Guillem Alenyà.

                FrailWatch
                

                  A robotic frailty assessment system for older adults
                

              

              

                
                
                Accomplishments
                
    
                  The aging of the population is a global phenomenon that has been increasing in recent years due to a decrease in fertility rates and an increase in life expectancy. This trend has significant implications for health, social welfare, economic development, as well as the provision of healthcare services in the region. Spain and Catalonia are no exception to this trend. Older adults are more likely to suffer from chronic diseases and require more medical attention. Frailty in elderly individuals is a key issue in the Catalan healthcare system, as it is a geriatric syndrome that increases vulnerability to stressful situations and the risk of developing disabilities, illnesses, and increased mortality. To address the challenges associated with frailty, the WHO has developed the framework of Integrated Care for Older People, which proposes tools for detecting frailty based on validated instruments and interventions to prevent frailty. However, these assessments require a series of resources (personnel and materials) that are difficult to assume in most care centers. Typically, primary healthcare professionals cannot collect these measures in a structured way because questionnaires and interviews require time, and data is collected manually and needs to be entered into digital formats by hand.

                  FRAILWATCH aims to promote the study of innovative social robotics capabilities with significant potential in the medical field. The observation and multimodal interaction capabilities of robots offer capabilities beyond existing digital solutions. The main objective of the project is to investigate the feasibility of using a social robot in the assessment of different parameters relevant to the assessment of intrinsic capacities and frailty of a patient.

                  Project funded by the 2023 Call for Research and Innovation Projects of the Barcelona City Council and the "La Caixa" Foundation.
